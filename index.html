About me

I am a Lecturer at Sorbonne University, where I work with the Machine Learning – Deep Learning and Information Access group within the Institute for Cognitive Systems and Artificial Intelligence (ICSAI). My research lies in the field of machine learning, with a strong focus on visual understanding and multimodal representation learning, including the fusion of text and image data as well as multi-sensor information. I am particularly engaged in the design of hybrid frameworks that integrate physical modeling with data-driven statistical methods. Another key aspect of my work is improving the robustness and generalization capabilities of learning systems so they remain reliable across diverse and challenging environments.

Before joining Sorbonne University, I served as an Assistant Professor at the European Center for Computational Intelligence and Data Science (ECCIDS) at the Conservatoire National des Arts et Métiers (CNAM), where I was part of the Vertigo Perception and Learning Group (VPLG).
