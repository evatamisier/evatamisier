About me

I'm a researcher and educator working at the intersection of artificial intelligence and music. My work explores how emerging technologies are reshaping the ways we create, experience, and understand sound. With a background in both music and computational research, I investigate topics such as generative music systems, human–machine collaboration, and the cultural implications of AI-driven creativity. My research combines technical experimentation with critical inquiry, examining not only what AI can produce, but also what it means for authorship, expression, and artistic identity. As a lecturer, I am committed to fostering interdisciplinary dialogue, encouraging students to approach technology as both a creative tool and a subject of reflection. My teaching bridges theory and practice, guiding students through hands-on exploration of machine learning tools while engaging with broader ethical and aesthetic questions. I regularly present my work at international conferences and contributes to collaborative projects that bring together artists, engineers, and researchers. Through my work, I aim to open new perspectives on the role of artificial intelligence in contemporary musical practices.
